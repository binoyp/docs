The Basic module is the foundation of every SecureLoad loading computer. It defines the vessel's loading condition and performs the two core compliance checks required for every cargo operation — intact stability and longitudinal strength.

---

## Loading Condition

A loading condition in SecureLoad is a complete snapshot of the vessel's weight distribution at a given moment. It is built from four categories of items:

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **Lightweight** | The vessel's own structural weight — fixed, taken from the approved lightweight survey |
| **Cargo** | All cargo items placed in holds, on deck, or in specific stowage positions |
| **Tanks** | Ballast water, fuel oil, diesel oil, fresh water, and other liquid loads with their filling levels |
| **Constants** | Stores, provisions, crew and effects, spare parts, and other deadweight items not elsewhere accounted for |

The sum of all four categories gives the **displacement** at that condition. Positions (longitudinal, transverse, vertical centres of gravity) are calculated for each item and combined to produce the overall centre of gravity **G** of the vessel.

## Intact Stability

Intact stability assessment checks whether the vessel, in its current loading condition, meets the mandatory stability criteria imposed by the IMO Intact Stability Code and the vessel's class-approved loading manual.

### Hydrostatic Calculations

From the displacement and trim, SecureLoad reads the vessel's hydrostatic tables to determine:

| Parameter | Symbol | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Draught (fwd, mid, aft) | T | Forward, midship, and aft draught |
| Displacement | Δ | Total mass of displaced water |
| Centre of Buoyancy | KB | Vertical centre of buoyancy |
| Metacentric Radius | BM | Distance from centre of buoyancy to transverse metacentre |
| Metacentric Height | GM | Measure of initial transverse stability; GM = KB + BM − KG |

### Free Surface Correction

For every partially filled tank, SecureLoad computes the free surface moment and applies the corresponding reduction to GM:

> **GM_effective = GM_solid − Σ (free surface moments) / Δ**

This correction is applied before any criteria are evaluated.

### GZ Curve

The righting lever **GZ** is computed at heel angles from 0° to 90° (or beyond, depending on vessel geometry). The curve describes the vessel's ability to return to upright after being heeled by an external force.

Key values read from the GZ curve:

| Parameter | Description |
|---|---|
| **GM** | Slope of the GZ curve at 0° (initial stability) |
| **GZ_max** | Maximum righting lever — the peak of the curve |
| **Angle of GZ_max** | Heel angle at which the maximum GZ occurs |
| **Angle of Vanishing Stability** | Heel angle at which GZ returns to zero |
| **Area 0°–30°** | Area under the GZ curve from 0° to 30° heel |
| **Area 0°–40°** | Area under the GZ curve from 0° to 40° heel |
| **Area 30°–40°** | Incremental area between 30° and 40° heel |

### Criteria Checks

SecureLoad evaluates the condition against the applicable stability criteria. Results are displayed as pass/fail with the calculated value alongside the required limit.

**IMO Intact Stability Code (2008 IS Code) — General Criteria:**

| Criterion | Limit |
|---|---|
| Area 0°–30° | ≥ 0.055 m·rad |
| Area 0°–40° | ≥ 0.090 m·rad |
| Area 30°–40° | ≥ 0.030 m·rad |
| GZ at 30° heel | ≥ 0.200 m |
| Angle of GZ_max | ≥ 25° |
| GM₀ (initial metacentric height) | ≥ 0.150 m |

Additional criteria — such as the **weather criterion**, grain stability requirements, or vessel-type-specific rules — are evaluated by the relevant optional modules.

## Longitudinal Strength

Longitudinal strength assessment verifies that the forces acting along the length of the hull remain within the class-approved permissible limits at all cross-sections of the vessel.

### Weight and Buoyancy Distribution

SecureLoad computes the distribution of:
- **Weight** — mass per unit length along the vessel, contributed by lightweight, cargo, tanks, and constants
- **Buoyancy** — upward hydrostatic force per unit length, derived from the vessel's form at the current draught and trim

### Load Curve

The difference between the weight and buoyancy distributions gives the **load curve** (net vertical force per unit length along the ship's length). Integrating the load curve yields:

| Derived Quantity | Description |
|---|---|
| **Shear Force (SF)** | Cumulative vertical force at each cross-section |
| **Bending Moment (BM)** | Cumulative moment at each cross-section — sagging (hull bends downward) or hogging (hull bends upward) |

### Permissible Limits

The class-approved permissible shear force and bending moment values are stored in the vessel's SecureLoad database. At every cross-section, the calculated value is compared against the permissible limit:

- Values below the limit: **pass**
- Values at or above the limit: **fail** — displayed with the percentage of utilisation

<Warning>
  Exceeding the permissible shear force or bending moment limits means the hull girder is being overstressed. A loading condition that fails longitudinal strength checks must not be executed.
</Warning>

## Draughts & Deadweight

In addition to stability and strength, the Basic module reports the complete draught summary:

| Output | Description |
|---|---|
| **Draught Forward** | Calculated draught at the forward perpendicular |
| **Draught Aft** | Calculated draught at the aft perpendicular |
| **Draught Midship** | Calculated draught amidships |
| **Trim** | Difference between aft and forward draughts (positive trim = trim by stern) |
| **Heel** | Transverse inclination of the vessel |
| **Displacement** | Total mass of the vessel in the current condition |
| **Deadweight** | Displacement minus lightweight — the total payload carried |
| **Freeboard** | Distance from the waterline to the main deck edge |

---

## Outputs

A loading condition calculated with the Basic module produces:

- **Stability summary** — GM, GZ curve plot, criteria check table (pass/fail)
- **Strength summary** — Shear force and bending moment diagrams with permissible limit overlays
- **Draught summary** — Fwd, mid, aft, trim, heel, displacement, deadweight
- **Loading condition report** — Printable/exportable document suitable for port state control, class surveyors, and onboard records
