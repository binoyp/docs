## Overview

The **Theoretical Reference** section provides the naval architecture and hydrostatics background behind the calculations performed by SecureLoad. It is intended for engineers, naval architects, and technically minded operators who want to understand the methods and assumptions applied by the system.

## Topics to Be Covered

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Hydrostatics" icon="water">
    Displacement, buoyancy, waterplane area, centers of buoyancy and gravity, and the fundamental hydrostatic relationships.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Intact Stability" icon="scale-balanced">
    Metacentric height (GM), the GZ righting lever curve, stability criteria (IMO IS Code, weather criterion), and area requirements.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Damage Stability" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Probabilistic and deterministic damage stability methods (SOLAS 2009), subdivision, and survivability assessment.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Longitudinal Strength" icon="ruler-horizontal">
    Hull girder loads — shear force and bending moment distributions, still water and wave-induced components, and permissible limits.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Trim & Draft" icon="arrows-up-down">
    Trim by the stern/head, draft corrections for water density, freeboard computation, and the effect of free surfaces.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Free Surface Effect" icon="droplet">
    Reduction of effective GM due to liquid free surfaces in tanks — calculation methods and SecureLoad's implementation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cargo & Tank Modeling" icon="boxes-stacked">
    How cargo holds, ballast tanks, and fuel tanks are modeled — volumetric capacity tables, ullage/sounding corrections.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Regulatory Framework" icon="shield-check">
    Overview of the IMO instruments, SOLAS chapters, and class society rules that govern loading computer requirements.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
